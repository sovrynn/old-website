The Final Parabola

I'm not sure how long this will take to play out. But I have a feeling it will take shape within a decade. Even sooner than that perhaps.

So, what is The Final Parabola?

Take a look at the current Bitcoin price chart, on a logarithmic scale:

BTC price chart

Notice how the price is shaped like a logarithmic curve?

Logarithmic Curve

Some people use this fact to extrapolate that the future Bitcoin price will follow a log curve forever:

Bitcoin Log Curve

The explanation given for this is typically diminishing returns and lengthening cycles. More specifically, that:

  • It becomes more difficult to lift Bitcoin's price higher the higher the Bitcoin price is
  • More time is required to drag in the larger amounts of money required to lift Bitcoin's price the higher the Bitcoin price is

However, I think the Bitcoin logarithmic curve model only works in the short-term, and will be wrong in the long-term. Instead, I think the Bitcoin price chart will eventually take this shape:

Flipped S-Curve

I'm not talking about the specific dimensions. I'm simply talking about the overall shape. While the logarithmic curve follows diminishing returns forever, the flipped S-curve shape I'm showing here reaches an inflection point where it turns into a parabola. This is what I'm referring to when I say "the final parabola".

The Final Parabola Labeled

So why do I think we will see a final parabola on the Bitcoin price chart?

First of all, the logarithmic curve model simply doesn't make sense to me when it comes to how I think Bitcoin will be adopted by the masses:

  • If the price just keeps making smaller and smaller gains over longer and longer cycles, that means adoption will get dragged on over longer and longer timeframes.
  • But we Bitcoiners all know Bitcoin is a viral, viral idea and that once people get it, adoption will spread like wildfire and eventually everyone will be selling everything they own to buy Bitcoin.
  • Q: So where's that mass hysteria and hype in the Bitcoin logarithmic curve price model?
  • A: It ain't there. It doesn't match up.

That's where my idea comes in. The reasoning for my idea begins with the adoption S-curve, which refers to the stages of adoption of a new product. Notice the inflection point labeled near the bottom of the curve in blue:

Adoption S-Curve

The S-curve becomes more interesting when we combine it with a population distribution of market participants:

Adopters S-Curve

Currently, I believe we are still in the "Innovators" section of Bitcoin adoption as money. Here's some justification for that:

  • Total wealth allocation to Bitcoin is not even 1%
  • Total number of wholecoiners in the world is nowhere close to 1%
  • No one I personally know has a significant Bitcoin allocation

How is the inflection point related to adoption?

So, what new force am I looking for? I'm looking for the inflection point.

The inflection point is where the adoption rate suddenly seems to change... from gradually, to suddenly. Sound familiar?

Take another look at this diagram. Notice how the adoption is really slow before the inflection point, but becomes significantly quicker after the inflection point?:

Adoption Rate Picking Up After Inflection Point

It may have taken a decade just to get through the "Innovators" stage and near the inflection point. But the rest of the adoption will happen at a much, much faster rate. Such is the parabolic nature of the S-curve, and the viral nature of Bitcoin.

What will this force of rapid adoption look like? I know exactly what it will look like - mass market euphoria. Animal spirits. Green candles. FOMO. That force that we are all so familiar with. The force that made all of us FOMO into Bitcoin in the first place.

What will the trigger be for this force to take over the world? Well, it turns out that FOMO is actually a self-reinforcing force. It started from nothing and created an ever-growing history of repeated Bitcoin ATHs. Even left untouched, it will take over the entire world on its own. However, a sudden loss of confidence around the world in the dollar system could be a catalyst that speeds it up. And in fact, a loss of confidence in the dollar system is exactly what motivated Satoshi to create Bitcoin. Funny how that works.

Anyways, regardless of the exact sequence of events, eventually the final parabola will happen. We know it'll happen because eventually the whole world will realize that they shouldn't have confidence in the dollar system. This will lead to the parabola where the whole world onboards to Bitcoin by FOMOing into it. The parabola which is depicted in the second half of the S-curve.
